# VISION.md – Product Vision & Value Proposition

> This document describes the "Why" behind the digital shopping list.
> It serves as the strategic compass for all product and development decisions.

---

## The Problem

Grocery shopping is a highly repetitive daily process. A typical household shops 1-3 times per week. Yet the process hasn't changed in decades: handwritten notes, forgotten items, inefficient walking through the store.

Existing shopping list apps merely digitize the note – they make the list digital, but not smarter.

## The Solution

A shopping list app that gets smarter with every shopping trip through machine learning. The core mechanism: the app learns the product order in the store and sorts the shopping list so the customer only needs to walk through the store once, from start to finish.

---

## Benefits for Customers

**Faster shopping trips.** The list follows the store's aisle order – no backtracking, no searching. Estimated savings: 5-10 minutes per trip, adding up to 8-16 hours per year.

**The app improves automatically.** First trip: default ALDI layout sorting. After 5 trips: the app knows the store's main zones. After 20 trips: product-level accuracy. The customer invests nothing extra – the app learns in the background.

**Family shopping made easy.** Shared lists let family members collect items continuously between shopping trips – not just on one day, but over days and weeks until the next trip. Everyone can add products from anywhere. Avatars show who added what.

**Shopping list without typing.** "Recent Purchases" shows products from the last 4 weeks – one tap to add. Weekly specials directly from the digital flyer. Barcode scanner: scan → instantly on the list. "Fill with typical products" creates a complete list based on purchase history. Future: voice assistants (Alexa, Google) add items – the app is only opened in the store.

**Cost planning.** Estimated total before shopping. Price updates via receipt scan.

**Relevant notifications.** "Notify me when kids' specials arrive." Alerts when regularly purchased products are discounted. Reminders of products bought last time at this store.

**Find products in-store.** Search for "olive oil" shows not just the price but where it is in the store. Future: store layout map with product location markers.

---

## Benefits for ALDI

**Customer acquisition and retention.** The app makes shopping more efficient – especially for families and large purchases. It's an active customer acquisition tool. The app becomes more valuable with every visit, increasing switching costs and binding customers to their regular store.

**Demand forecasting.** When a product appears on shopping lists significantly more or less often than usual, this is an early indicator of deviating demand. Order quantities can be adjusted proactively – before sell-through becomes visible in the store. Seasonal and regional trends become visible early.

**Planned vs. actual purchases.** When customers scan their receipts, the difference between the shopping list and actual purchase becomes visible. This enables calculation of substitution potential (Product X planned, Product Y bought) and cross-selling potential (unplanned additions).

**Assortment intelligence.** Search queries reveal what customers want but ALDI doesn't carry – exposing gaps in the assortment. Loyalty-level data without registration barriers. Targeted marketing based on purchase behavior.

**More efficient store operations.** Faster shopping → less congestion during peak hours. Customers move in one direction through the store → less counter-traffic in narrow aisles. Fewer parking spots needed at same revenue (shorter dwell time per customer).

**In-store product finder.** The app doubles as a product locator: search "olive oil" → "next to the wine section" or aisle number. Could run on a customer-facing iPad in-store as a self-service terminal.

**Direct customer channel.** No dependency on third-party apps (Google, Apple, Bring!). Direct digital touchpoint before, during and after shopping. Push capability for specials and personalized offers.

**Digital flyer replaces print.** Higher conversion: from browsing to purchase is one tap. Measurable: which specials actually end up on shopping lists.

**Crowdsourced store mapping.** Customers map the shelf structure of every store simply by shopping normally. No manual layout maintenance needed. Self-corrects after store renovations within a few shopping trips.

**Competitive advantage.** No other discounter offers a self-learning shopping list. Data moat: the algorithm improves with more data – imitators start from zero. Fits ALDI's philosophy: simple, efficient, customer-focused.

## Value Chain

```
Customer uses app → Shopping data flows back anonymized →
  → Sorting improves (for the customer)
  → Assortment insights for ALDI (for the business)
  → Specials communicated more effectively (for both sides)
```

The customer doesn't "give away" data – they receive immediate, tangible value in return (better sorting, time savings). This distinguishes the app from pure data collection approaches.

## Core Principles

1. **Time savings above all.** Every feature must pass: "Does this save the customer time while shopping?" If not, it's not a priority.

2. **Learn, don't configure.** The customer shouldn't need to set anything up. The app learns automatically through normal use.

3. **Simplicity over feature count.** ALDI customers value simplicity. The app must be understandable in 10 seconds. Better to have few features that work perfectly than many mediocre ones.

4. **No gamification.** No points, badges, levels. The value is the time savings itself.
